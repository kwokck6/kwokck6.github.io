Introduction

I have always been fascinated by the process that the members of the public get to vote their representatives in the legislature and/or head of government. This motivated me to learn more about Hong Kong's election system, and I have written an Independent Enquiry Study (IES) for compulsory voting. That may hardly be something interesting for most people, but I am still keen to learn more about psephology (the study of election). After I learned about machine learning and deep learning, this idea came to my mind: 'What if we can use AI to predict the election results?'

Note:

This project was submitted as a course project of AIST4010 in 2021/22 Term 2, CUHK. Further development of this project is scheduled.

Related works

This project is inspired by P. Gilbert's and V. Jayawickrama's work, both of which are published on Towards Data Science. The hierarchical GNN idea stems from Deep Hierarchical Graph Convolution for Election Prediction from Geospatial Census Data written by Li et al.

Methodology

We propose the following methods for predicting the election results:

  1. Method 1: Use simple machine learning models
  2. Method 2: Use graph neural networks
  3. Method 3: Use hierarchical GNN
